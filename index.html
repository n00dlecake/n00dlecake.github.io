Mahere Mahi Toi

You can‚Äôt travel far around the Otago Polytechnic without stumbling across one of the many art installations that populate our campus. Until now there has been no resource which allows people to navigate this carefully curated collection of artwork. Mahere Mahi Toi has been developed in collaboration with the Dunedin School of Art to address this opportunity.

As an application Mahere Mahi Toi features an interactive map, curated routes, and insights into the works and artists behind them, providing users with not only a wayfinding tool but also a deeper connection to the creative and cultural elements of our campus.

Kia ora,

I currently a student in the Otago Polytechnic Honours Programme with a Bachelor of Communication Design. I'm fortunate enough to have dabbled in a diverse collection of fields, including, tertiary lecturing, health sciences, trade, beauty therapy, hospitality, management, and retail. While varied, all of these experiences have bolstered my communication skills and given me a unique lens to wield when engaging in problem solving.

I see myself as someone who is fueled by eclectic creativity with a genuine appreciation for authenticity. I like to approach challenges enthusiastically, and with as much relevant research as I can to aid in efficiently finding solutions, always aiming to transform them into opportunities.

My commitment to learning is core to who I am, which helps to quickly pick up new skills. Embracing biculturalism and sustainability as guiding principles, I strive towards genuine innovation, consistently redefining what my best looks like.

I'm open to opportunities and flexible about my future so don’t hesitate to get in touch if you have a creative goal that we can achieve together.
